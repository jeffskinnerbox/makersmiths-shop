name: edit-article
description: Edit and improve articles by restructuring sections, improving clarity, and tightening prose. Use when user wants to edit, revise, or improve an article draft.
---

Separate editing from writing — treat them as distinct passes, not simultaneous.

## Level 1 — Structure (read before touching anything)

Read the full draft without making edits. Identify:

- **Thesis**: What is the article's core claim or purpose?
- **Main points**: What arguments or sections support it?
- **Dependencies**: Information is a DAG — earlier sections must not assume knowledge introduced later. Flag any ordering violations.
- **Gaps**: Missing arguments, out-of-place sections, or tonal inconsistencies.

Present this analysis to the user: proposed section order, identified gaps, any reordering needed. Confirm before proceeding.

## Level 2 — Organization, Development, Flow

Work section by section after user confirms the structure.

**Organization**
- Reorder paragraphs within a section into logical sequence.
- Move or delete sentences that belong elsewhere or add no value.

**Development**
- Identify underdeveloped points (claims without support, examples, or significance).
- Add supporting details where weak; cut where redundant.

**Flow**
- Open each paragraph with a topic sentence; close with a transition that bridges to the next.
- Prefer active voice. Break up sentences longer than ~30 words.
- Cut filler: "that," excess prepositions, hedging phrases ("kind of," "sort of," "in order to").
- Keep consistent word choice for key terms throughout.

## Level 3 — Copyediting and Proofreading

**Copyediting**
- Fix awkward phrasing and comma splices.
- Split paragraphs exceeding 240 characters.
- Verify consistent voice, tone, and spelling.
- Format headlines and titles per publication style.

**Proofreading**
- Read the final draft aloud (or simulate it) to catch errors invisible on silent read.
- Check punctuation, grammar, and spelling in one final pass.

## Tips

- Take breaks between levels; fresh eyes catch what familiarity misses.
- Do not start Level 2 until Level 1 structure is confirmed with the user.
- Do not merge levels — completing each fully before moving on prevents rework.
